Club road race championship update following the Manchester marathon...

As expected, we had a huge turnout of runners at Manchester, with 26 registered Altrincham Athletes completing the course in glorious sunny and still conditions. As at the Wilmslow HM, Michael Berks and Ben Walmsley made up the front two, this time with their positions reversed, Michael finishing in 2:34:03 and Ben shortly after in 2:34:41, both personal bests (Michael's a huge 19 minutes improvement on his last marathon 13 years ago). Jo Rosenberg was first Altrincham lady in a brilliant run of 2:56:21 to be second FV50 overall. An achievement made all the more impressive by the fact Manchester was an official representative race for home countries masters series and so had many of the best age-group athletes from England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland competing. However, her impressive Age % score of 87.30% was narrowly beaten by Richard Evans, with his superb new personal best of 2:54:40 as an MV60 scoring 87.68%. Full times and Age-% scores here: https://altrincham-athletics.github.io/Road-Race-Championship/races/manchester-marathon_summary.html

In terms of the overall marathon table, Michael and Ben's races slot them in to 3rd and 4th in the table, with Michael Berks' time just 4 seconds slower than Michael Whitham's 2:33:59 in Seville - a difference that could prove crucial at the end of the year! Michael Whitham it should be noted was selflessly on 2:50 pacing duties at Manchester, running a metronomic 2:49:21 and no doubt helping many runners to new PBs on the day. Jo Rosenberg's time was 7 seconds faster than her run at New York, keeping her first female but not enough to improve her position in the Age % rankings, where she is now third behind the new leader Richard Evans and Vicki Perry. Finally, congratulations to Duncan Dickinson, whose 3:45:33 at Manchester means he joins Paul Abraham as members with a full set of 6 scoring races for the year.

Following Manchester, in the overall championship table, we now have a two-way tie at the top and five athletes all on five races, separated by just 12 points! A healthy score of 44 points from the marathon table brings Michael Berks level with Becky Hansen on 235 points. In another tie just 11 points adrift, Richard Evans' 39 marathon points means he joins Michael Whitham on 224 points, with Ben Walmsley (41 marathon points) just a point behind on 223.

To say things are set up nicely for the final race at Alderley Edge 10k would be an understatement! Before then, there are one or two other things to watch...

Any 5ks can still score, so there is the chance to improve on an existing time, or in Michael Whitham's case, record their first of this championship. There is also London marathon next weekend, and while none of the leading protagonists are racing, a high Age-% score from another athlete (Tom Sullivan, looking at you) could make a difference. All to play for...
